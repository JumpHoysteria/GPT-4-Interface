Bean Chili Recipe:<br><br>Ingredients:<br>- 2 tablespoons olive oil<br>- 1 large onion, chopped<br>- 1 green bell pepper, chopped<br>- 1 red bell pepper, chopped<br>- 2 cloves garlic, minced<br>- 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and minced (optional)<br>- 2 cups vegetable broth<br>- 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes<br>- 1 can (15 ounces) black beans, drained and rinsed<br>- 1 can (15 ounces) kidney beans, drained and rinsed<br>- 1 can (15 ounces) pinto beans, drained and rinsed<br>- 1 cup frozen corn<br>- 2 tablespoons chili powder<br>- 1 tablespoon ground cumin<br>- 1 teaspoon dried oregano<br>- 1 teaspoon paprika<br>- Salt and black pepper, to taste<br>- Optional toppings: chopped cilantro, avocado, green onions, shredded cheese, sour cream<br><br>Instructions:<br><br>1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, green bell pepper, and red bell pepper. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are softened.<br><br>2. Add the minced garlic and jalapeno pepper (if using) to the pot. Cook, stirring constantly, for 1 minute, or until fragrant.<br><br>3. Stir in the vegetable broth, crushed tomatoes, black beans, kidney beans, pinto beans, and frozen corn. Mix well to combine.<br><br>4. Add the chili powder, ground cumin, dried oregano, and paprika to the pot. Stir well to incorporate the spices into the chili mixture.<br><br>5. Bring the chili to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, and let it simmer uncovered for 30 minutes, or until the chili has thickened and the flavors have melded together. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.<br><br>6. Season the chili with salt and black pepper to taste.<br><br>7. Serve the chili hot, with your choice of toppings such as chopped cilantro, avocado, green onions, shredded cheese, or sour cream.<br><br>8. Enjoy your homemade bean chili!
